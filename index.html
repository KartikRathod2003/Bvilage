About the Village

Welcome to
Banjara Tanda

Banjara Tanda is a vibrant village nestled in the Mahur Taluka of Nanded District, in the culturally rich state of Maharashtra, India. It lies in the heart of the Marathwada region under the Aurangabad Division.

The village is located 9 km from Mahur city — the nearest urban centre — and approximately 121 km from the district headquarters of Nanded. Its PIN code is 431721, under the postal head office of Mahore (Nanded).

"Tanda" — a word rooted in Banjara tradition — refers to a settlement or encampment of the Banjara community, one of India's most colourful and historically significant nomadic peoples.

The village is administered by a Sarpanch, the elected head of the Gram Panchayat, as per the Panchayati Raj Act. Banjara Tanda has a literacy rate of approximately 54.96%, with ongoing government and community initiatives to improve education and infrastructure.

Heritage & Culture

The Banjara Community

🏺 Who Are the Banjaras?

The Banjara are one of India's most historically significant nomadic communities, traditionally known as pastoralists, long-distance traders, and transporters of goods. The word "Banjara" is believed to derive from the Sanskrit "vana chara" — meaning "wanderers of the forest."

🌐 Language & Identity

The Banjara community speaks Gor-Boli (also called Lambadi), a distinctive language that blends Rajasthani, Sanskrit and local dialects. They refer to themselves as Gor and outsiders as Kor, maintaining a strong sense of cultural identity.

🪔 Tanda – The Heart of Community

A Tanda is the traditional Banjara settlement, historically a caravan of bullocks that evolved into a permanent village cluster. Each Tanda is governed by a Naik (headman) and a Gor Panchayat (tribal council) that settles disputes and upholds community customs.

👗 Vibrant Cultural Traditions

Banjara women are famous for their brilliantly embroidered clothing, heavy silver jewellery, and colourful attire. Their folk art, music, and dance traditions are an integral part of Maharashtra's cultural heritage. The Mahur Museum nearby displays authentic Banjara artefacts and cultural items.

ℹ️
Did You Know? Banjara Tanda in Mahur Taluka is part of a rich cultural corridor in Nanded District. The famous Mahur Devi Temple — a Shaktipeeth — is located near Mahur city, making this region an important spiritual and cultural destination in Marathwada.
